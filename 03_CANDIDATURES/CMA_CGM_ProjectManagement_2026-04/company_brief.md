# company_brief - CMA CGM

## About the Company

- **Sector**: Maritime shipping & logistics (3rd largest container shipping group globally)
- **Headquarters**: Marseille, France (home city for Fabrice - strong advantage)
- **Ownership**: Family-owned (Saadé family) - French institution, strong loyalty culture
- **Scale**: 400+ vessels, 160+ shipping lines, operations in 160+ countries, ~160,000 employees globally
- **Subsidiaries**: CEVA Logistics (contract logistics), La Méridionale, Air France Cargo stake, and others
- **Revenue**: ~€52B (2023) - one of the most profitable shipping cycles in history post-COVID

## Customer Care & Digital Transformation Strategy

- **eBusiness platform**: real-time cargo tracking, customisable dashboard, end-to-end digital B2B experience - moving from port-to-port to door-to-door intelligence
- **Singulier partnership**: CMA CGM partnered with UX/digital agency Singulier to reposition digital as a value driver and redesign the entire customer experience - predictive insights, container-level tracking
- **AI & IoT at the core**: R&D, IoT, artificial intelligence and blockchain deployed to create smart, secure logistics solutions and more intuitive digital tools
- **2026 ambitions**: stepping into 2026 with explicit ambition to accelerate digital and operational transformation across the group

## Supply Chain & Transformation Priorities

- Scaling digital customer experience globally (from booking to delivery)
- Knowledge management and standardisation across a highly fragmented global organisation
- AI and automation to reduce manual customer service touchpoints
- Cross-functional integration: connecting business, IT, and operations teams around the customer journey
- Onboarding and upskilling large global teams on new tools and processes

## Why This Role is Strategic

The Global Project Management Specialist is a high-visibility connector role - translating CMA CGM's digital transformation strategy into operational reality for customer care. The person in this role drives the programmes, builds the knowledge frameworks, measures the KPIs, and bridges the gap between business intent and IT execution. In a group of 160,000 people across 160+ countries, this kind of structured delivery capability is scarce and highly valued.
